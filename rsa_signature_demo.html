Why It Works

Signσ = m^d mod n
Verifyσ^e mod n =? m
CorrectnessEuler: m^φ(n) ≡ 1 (mod n)

Because e·d ≡ 1 (mod φ(n)), raising m to the d-th power and then to the e-th power is a full trip around the group of order φ(n), landing back on m. Changing even one digit of m or σ breaks this identity — that's why tampering is caught.

Uses small primes for hand-checkable arithmetic — real RSA uses primes hundreds of digits long.
